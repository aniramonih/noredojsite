Who is Eun Yang?

Eun Yang is a Korean-American television reporter. Currently, she is serving as a news reporter in the morning news for WRC-T. In addition, she also served for WUSA as a television reporter later worked as an anchor. 

Eun Yang was born on 13th June 1976, in Seoul, South Korea. Born to Korean parents, she belongs to Asian ethnicity and holds dual nationality (Korean-American). However, details regarding her parents and family background are still unknown. 

Looking towards her educational background, Yang studied at Paint Branch High School located in Burtonsville, Maryland. Later, she attended the University of Maryland and received her degree in broadcast journalism. 

With a dream of being a journalist since childhood, Eun kicked-off her journalism career during her days in the University of Maryland. She began as a reporter for Maryland update and later worked as a production assistant for WUSA. 

In order to pursue her career professionally, Yang joined National Geographic Channel and began serving as the correspondent for the National Geographic Today. 

Currently, she is working as a reporter for NBC 4. Moreover, she also recognized as one of the most praised news reporters. 

In addition, Eun also serves on the Board of Visitors for the Philip Merrill College of Journalism. Yang is ranked as the no.1 news reporter in the U.S. She has also received an Emmy Award for her brilliant performance.
