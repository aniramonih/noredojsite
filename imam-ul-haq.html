Imam-ul-Haq is a rising left-handed middle-order Pakistani batsman who is perhaps best known for his flamboyant stroke-play. His career on Pakistan’s national cricket team flourished from day one itself. He managed to score a century on his debut “One Day International.” Over a short period of time, he has also managed to amass a huge fan base with more than 200k followers on Twitter and more than 100k followers on Instagram.

Imam-ul-Haq Facts

  • Prior to moving to Pakistan in 1947, Imam’s ancestors belonged to the city of Hansi.
  • Imam had developed a passion for the sport at an early age and credits his uncle Inzamam-ul-Haq for it.
  • His brother played cricket in England while he was still young.
  • While studying his O-levels, Imam switched to Matric and scored a total of 940 marks. This, in turn, earned him a scholarship to study at the Punjab College.
  • Whilst playing cricket, Imam also went to NCA training in the mornings and studied for 1 hour each day during his schooling days.
  • Imam played his first publicly viewed cricket debut in 2012 at the “U19 Asia Cup”.
  • He is a huge admirer of the Multan Cricket Stadium in Pakistan and had been going there ever since he was a kid.
  • Imam is close friends with cricketer Babar Azam.
  • During his debut ODI match with Sri Lanka on October 18, 2017, Imam became the second Pakistani batsman after former cricketer Saleem Elahi to score a century on his debut.
  • Over a span of 3 years, Imam had played 2 “U19 World Cups” for Pakistan.
  • He claims to be a short-tempered man but is working on improving it.
  • In July 2019, Imam was dragged into a social media controversy after screenshots of his chat with a girl were leaked on Twitter. In it, Imam is allegedly said to have been in a relationship with multiple women at the same time. Later, the PCB MD Wasim Khan revealed that Imam is sorry and has apologized for his wrongdoings.
  • During the 3rd “One Day Innings” with South Africa, Imam became the second-fastest batsman to score 1,000 runs in ODIs. He managed to achieve the milestone during his 19th inning.
  • On May 5, 2019, Imam made his “Twenty20 International” debut for Pakistan in a match against England.
