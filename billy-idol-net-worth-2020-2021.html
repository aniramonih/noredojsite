Early Life

William Michael Albert Broad was born on 30 November 1955 in Stanmore, Middlesex, England. He is the son of William Alfred Broad and Joan Broad. He has one younger sister named Jane Broad. His family moved to Patchogue, New York, when he was just two years old. After four years, his family returned to England. Later in 1971, his family settled in Bromley, London.

Billy went to Ravensbourne School for Boys in the London Borough of Bromley. After that, he continued his studies at Worthing High School for Boys in West Sussex. He always has been a huge fan of rock music. His stage name “Billy Idol” was inspired by one of his teacher’s remark during his school time. Billy also went to Sussex University for his bachelor’s degree in 1975, but due to his interest in music, he was drop out of college after one year. After that, he joined the local band of the Bromley Contingent of Sex Pistols fans.

Career

Billy Idol started off his career as a guitarist in a punk rock band Chelsea in 1975. Later, Billy and his bandmate Tony James left the band and co-founded a band named Generation X. The Generation X band became to be one of the few punk bands who got the chance to perform on the famous BBC Television’s music chart television program TOTP. After that, the band was signed by Chrysalis Records and released three commercial hit albums. In 1981, Billy left the Band and moved to New York to try his luck in a solo career.

In 1982, Billy released his first solo album “Billy Idol,” which turns out to be a major hit of that time. His album was ranked at #45 positions on the list of Billboard 200. It was also awarded a gold certification by the Recording Industry Association of America.

Billy’s second studio album, “Rebel Yell,” was released in 1983. The album was commercially successful hit as it was ranked at 6th position on the Billboard 200 after that, Billy has given several hit albums like Cyberpunk, Devil’s Playground, Happy Holidays, and Kings & Queens of the Underground.

Awards & Achievement

Billy Idol has won many awards and nominations for his incredible work. His blockbuster hit single “Cradle of Love” has won ASCAP Film and Television Music Award in 1991. He has also won the “MTV Video Music Awards” in the category of Best Video from a Film in 1990. Apart from that, he was also nominated 13 times for the prominent awards included Grammy Awards.
