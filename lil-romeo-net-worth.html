What is Lil Romeo’s Net Worth?

Lil Romeo is a multi-talented American artist with a net worth of $5 million. He is known for his skills in rapping, acting, basketball, entrepreneurship, and modeling.

He rose to fame in the early 2000s when he signed with No Limit Records, a label founded by his father, Master P.

Percy Romeo Miller: A Talented Musician from a Musical Family

Percy Romeo Miller was born on August 19, 1989, in New Orleans, Louisiana. His parents, Master P and Sonya C, were both successful rappers in their time. Romeo is part of a musical family, with his uncle, C-Murder, and cousin, Mo B. Dick, also in the music industry. He is known for his talents as a musician and performer, and was previously known by the stage name Lil Romeo. His sister, Cymphonique Miller, is also a singer and actress.

Romeo’s Career Highlights

Romeo began his career at the age of five, signing to Soulja Music Entertainment, a subsidiary of No Limit Records. His debut album “Lil’ Romeo” (2001) included the hit single “My Baby,” and peaked at number 6 on the Billboard 200. His second album “Game Time” (2002) sold 2.5 million copies in the US. Romeo founded record label Guttar Music Entertainment with his father in 2005, releasing his first solo project “Lottery” in 2006. He continued to release music, forming the group Miller Boyz in 2007 and the record label The Next Generation Entertainment in 2008. Romeo has won three Billboard Awards, including Rap Artist of the Year in 2001.

Romeo’s Ventures Beyond Music

Romeo pursued a career in acting, making his debut in “Max Keeble’s Big Move” in 2001. He went on to star alongside Jessica Alba in “Honey” in 2003 and appeared in several other films such as “Jumping the Broom” and “Madea’s Witness Protection.” He also had his own Nickelodeon sitcom, “Romeo!,” from 2003 to 2006. Romeo was a contestant on “Dancing With the Stars” in 2011 and has appeared on other reality shows such as “The Millionaire Matchmaker” and “Celebrity Fear Factor.” He currently hosts the MTV reality show “Ex on the Beach.”

In addition to his acting and hosting gigs, Romeo launched his own clothing line, College Boyys, in 2010 and has starred in commercials for McDonald’s and ICDC Colleges. He also had a successful basketball career, playing for several high schools and the University of Southern California.
