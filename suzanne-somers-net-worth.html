What is the Net Worth of Suzanne Somers in 2024?

Suzanne Somers had an estimated net worth of $100 million at the time of her death in 2023. This substantial wealth stemmed from her successful career in acting, authorship, and entrepreneurship.

In comparison to her co-stars, Suzanne’s net worth was significantly higher. Angela Watson is estimated to have a net worth of around $3 million, Priscilla Barnes about $1.5 million, Christine Lakin roughly $2 million, and Joyce DeWitt approximately $4 million.

Suzanne’s diversified income sources, including the famous ThighMaster, and her real estate investments played a significant role in her financial success.

Major Career Milestones Contributing to Suzanne Somers’ Wealth

Suzanne Somers’ journey to wealth began with her role as Chrissy Snow on Three’s Company. Despite initial earnings of $5,000 per episode, her salary was eventually raised to $30,000.

However, seeking parity with her male co-star, she demanded $150,000 per episode and a share of the profits. Her departure from the show due to these demands was a significant turning point.

After Three’s Company, Suzanne starred in Step by Step, further bolstering her career. She also became a prominent figure in Las Vegas performances and had a brief stint on Broadway.

Beyond acting, her entrepreneurial spirit shone through with the ThighMaster, a product that generated over $100 million in revenue.

Suzanne Somers as an Author

Suzanne wrote over 25 books, many of which became best-sellers. Her works on health and wellness, including books like Ageless, added to her income and solidified her position as a respected author.

Real Estate Investments and Transactions

Suzanne and her husband, Alan Hamel, were savvy real estate investors. They owned a mansion in Palm Springs and several oceanfront properties in Malibu.

FAQs about Suzanne Somers

How did Suzanne Somers die?

Suzanne Somers passed away on October 15, 2023, after a long battle with breast cancer. She had been fighting the disease for over 23 years.

Was she included in the Oscars In Memoriam segment?

No, Suzanne Somers was notably left out of the Oscars In Memoriam segment in 2024. However, her husband Alan Hamel expressed that the fan response and outpouring of love were more meaningful to her memory.

What are some of Suzanne Somers’ most famous roles?

She is best known for her roles as Chrissy Snow on Three’s Company and Carol Foster Lambert on Step by Step.

Did she have any children?

Yes, she had one son, Bruce Somers Jr., from her first marriage to Bruce Somers.

What kind of business ventures was she involved in?

She had a successful line of health and wellness products, including skincare and fitness items. She also authored several best-selling books on health and wellness.

How did Suzanne Somers start her career?

She began her career with minor roles in films and television shows in the late 1960s. Her breakout role came with George Lucas’s film American Graffiti, followed by her iconic role in Three’s Company.

What controversies did she face?

She faced controversy for her outspoken views against chemotherapy and her advocacy for alternative cancer treatments, which drew criticism from medical professionals.

Did Suzanne Somers receive any awards or honors?

While she did not receive major acting awards, she was beloved by fans and remained a popular figure in entertainment and health industries until her passing. Her legacy includes her influence on wellness and fitness culture.

What was her approach to health and wellness?

She promoted a chemical-free and organic lifestyle, focusing on natural health and wellness. She wrote extensively about these topics and developed a range of related products.
