Jed McCaleb Biography

Jed Mccaleb was born on June 8, 1975, in Fayetteville, Arkansas, USA. His family later relocated to Little Rock, Arkansas, where he spent the majority of his childhood. McCaleb is not the type of person who is open about their family history. He has not revealed who his parents are or whether he has any siblings. He enjoyed technological activities as a child, and it was his dream job after finishing his education.

He enrolled at the University of California, Berkeley, after graduating from high school. However, his desire to become a technician prevented him from completing his education, and he dropped out while still a freshman. He relocated to New York and began his career as a tech entrepreneur.

Career

Jed McCaleb founded his first company, MetalMachine Inc., in 2000, where the eDonkey2000 application was first used. In 2001, he was joined by Sam Yagan, who was appointed CEO while still serving as the company’s CTO. He also created Overnet, a network that collaborated with eDonkey2000 and attracted over 4 million users. However, in 2006, the company agreed with the RIAA to stop the distribution of these networks in exchange for paying the RIAA $30 million to protect them from copyright.

In 2007, he purchased Mtgox.com, which he later changed to bitcoin in 2010, and the website began to grow. In February, he sold it to Mark Karpeles, remaining a minority owner. The company went bankrupt in 2014.

In 2011, McCaleb created the Ripple Protocol, a digital currency. Rather than using bitcoin, this system relied on consensus to verify transactions. He appointed Arthur Britto as chief strategist and Chris Larsen as CEO. In 2013, he stepped down from his active role in the company.

In 2014, he and his partner Joyce Kim co-founded Stellar Development Foundation, which enabled cross-border monetary transactions. It began operations on July 31, 2014, with a $3 million loan from Stripe. He currently serves as the company’s CTO. In the same building as Steller, he launched Lightyear.io, which was linked together to allow global currency exchange. Lightyear later collaborated with IBM. Lightyear merged with Chain.com in September 2018 and was renamed Interstellar.
