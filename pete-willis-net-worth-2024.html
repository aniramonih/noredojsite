Early Life and Career Beginnings

Pete Willis was born in Sheffield, England. He developed a passion for music at a young age and began playing the guitar in his early teens. In 1977, he co-founded Def Leppard with his schoolmates, and they quickly gained popularity in the UK’s rock scene.

Willis’s guitar work and songwriting skills played a significant role in the band’s early success. However, his time with the band was cut short due to personal issues, and he was asked to leave in 1982.
