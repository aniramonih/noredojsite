Alexee Trevizo Mom: Questions Raised On A Mothers Upbringing

Posted by Billy Koelling on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

The spotlight suddenly falls upon Alexee Trevizo mom as questions emerge regarding her role in the upbringing that led to the horrific crime conducted by her daughter,  which shocked the entire community.

Alexee Trevizo, a 19-year-old woman from New Mexico, has been charged with first-degree murder and tampering with evidence after authorities discovered the baby’s body in a trash can. 

The circumstances surrounding this heartbreaking incident have raised questions and sparked a legal battle that is now unfolding.

The events leading up to the discovery of the deceased infant began when Trevizo visited the emergency room in late December due to back pain. 

Medical tests revealed that she was pregnant and in labor, prompting Trevizo to lock herself in a bathroom. 

There, she gave birth to a baby boy on January 27th. However, instead of seeking medical assistance or notifying hospital staff, she placed the baby in a bag and concealed it among other trash.

Tragically, by the time the newborn was found, he had already passed away, and Trevizo had left the hospital.

 An autopsy confirmed that the cause of death was homicide, setting the stage for the serious charges she now faces.

Gary C. Mitchell, Trevizo’s attorney, has asserted that his client should not be charged with murder, citing her lack of criminal history. 

He claims that there are significant discrepancies surrounding what transpired at the hospital, suggesting that this case does not fit the profile of a classic child abuse scenario.

Mitchell’s defense strategy likely involves scrutinizing the events leading up to and following the birth, aiming to establish a narrative that may weaken the charges against Trevizo. 

The forthcoming court hearing on Friday, 19th May, 2023, is scheduled to review conditions of release, and will shed further light on the legal trajectory of this case.

The body cam footage captures an intensely heartbreaking moment as medical staff, police officers, and Alexee Trevizo mom confront a 19-year-old patient.

The doctor’s words hang heavy in the air, “We discovered a lifeless baby in the bathroom.”

With a trembling voice, Trevizo asserts that it was her baby, confessing her overwhelming confusion and desperation. 

In response, her mother reminds her daughter of previous discussions, urging her to embrace honesty during this devastating ordeal.

Trevizo overcomes as she desperately insists that the baby didn’t cry, giving us a glimpse into the intense and confusing feelings that overwhelmed her, making it difficult for her to think clearly during that critical time.

Alexee’s mom expresses shock and frustration, questioning her daughter’s actions and referencing similar cases where mothers faced legal consequences for harming their newborns. 

The video emphasizes the distress and confusion surrounding the situation, with Trevizo stating that she was scared and believed the baby was not crying.

This case bears similarities to another recent incident in New Mexico, where a teenage mother was convicted of attempted murder for placing her newborn son in a plastic bag and discarding him in a dumpster. 

In both instances, the young mothers claimed ignorance and panic upon learning of their pregnancies, leading to tragic outcomes.
