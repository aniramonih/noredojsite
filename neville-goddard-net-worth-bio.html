Neville Goddard lectures

Neville Goddard settled in Los Angeles in the 1950s after traveling extensively throughout the US.

Neville Goddard’s The Law and The Promise

Starting in 1959, Neville began to experience what he called “The Promise,” which he claimed was Scriptural and lasted for 1,260 days.

It included the cosmology of union with the Godhead after death and future restoration for those who did not accept The Promise in life.

Neville claimed that The Law was given by God to cushion the blows of life, but the ultimate goal was to fulfill Scripture through The Promise.

In the later part of the 1960s and early 1970s, he gave more importance to The Promise than to The Law, stating that while The Law could change circumstances, it was only temporary.

The Promise, on the other hand, was unconditional and could not be earned.

In his final years, Neville believed that his work was done, and he was eager to depart for the next life, where he would know his loved ones as before, for they were all brothers infinitely in love with each other.
