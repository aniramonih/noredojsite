Where was Richard Carpenter born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Richard Carpenter, an American citizen, was born to parents Harold Bertram Carpenter and Agnes Reuwer Tatum on October 15, 1946, in New Haven, Connecticut. Richard is his parents’ first and only child. Richard’s older sister, Karen, came along four years later, and they grew up together.

Richard and his family moved to Los Angeles, California, when his sister turned 13, and he enrolled at California State University, Los Angeles. His sibling was a Downey High School Bulldog.

Richard and Karen both loved music and considered becoming famous musicians at one point in their lives.

Siblings

The siblings finally found success in the music industry when, after several failed attempts, they created a duo band called Carpenters in 1969. Richard and Karen released 10 studio albums and a string of great songs throughout their tenure together, including “Top of the World,” “Only Yesterday,” “Superstar,” “Ticket to Ride,” and many more.

Over the years, the duo has amassed record sales of over 100 million. However, after 14 years together, the band broke up due to the stress of popularity.

Quaalude Causes Sister’s Anorexia and Death

Richard, who is tall at six feet, used his newfound fame to indulge in a rock star lifestyle, complete with excessive drinking and drug use. Trouble in his personal and professional life resulted from his addiction to the hypnotic and tranquil drug Quaalude. He spent six weeks in a recovery facility before he was able to overcome his addiction.

Karen, his sister, had problems in her teens due to anorexia. She began using Ipecac, a medicine known to induce vomiting, in an attempt to control her weight, but it ultimately led to her death.

On February 4, 1983, at the age of 32, she took an overdose of Ipecac and died of cardiac failure.

Is Richard Carpenter Single? Relationship
