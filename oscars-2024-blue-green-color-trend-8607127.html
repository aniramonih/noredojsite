The Color Trend That Swept the Red Carpet

Posted by Patria Henriques on Tuesday, August 13, 2024

The best-dressed celebs wore a rainbow of colors at the 2024 Oscars, but two colors in particular made a splash on the red carpet: baby blues and soft, seafoam greens. While there were still plenty of bold blacks and shimmering metallics to walk down the step and repeat, we found these watery hues refreshing from the heavier hues.

Lupita Nyong'o in a Giorgio Armani Privé sequined dress and Da'vine Joy Randolph in a frilly Louis Vuitton frock twinned in similar blues. At the same time, actors like Taylor Zakhar Perez also took note of the color trend with his powdery Prada suit. Designer Elie Saab served up a frothy seafoam-colored dress for Hailee Steinfeld that floated across the red carpet, while Emma Stone stood out in a sophisticated and structured Louis Vuitton look rendered in soft green.

See ahead for the most beautiful blue and glamorous green looks from the 2024 Oscars red carpet.

Lupita Nyong'o in Armani Privé

Presenter Lupita Nyong'o selected a custom Giorgio Armani Privé powdery blue look that was comprised of silk embroidery, embellished crystal beading, and a deep V-neckline, which was balanced by a pile of pale blue feathers at the peplum waist and flared skirt hem.

Emma Stone in Louis Vuitton

Winner of the Best Actress award for her work in Poor Things, Stone chose to wear a minty cloqué seashell jacquard gown with a tulip-constructed bustier, which made a bold style statement. She polished off her look with high-end jewelry from the brand.

Hailee Steinfeld in Elie Saab

French design house Elie Saab created a flowy, frothy evening gown for Hailee Steinfeld. The actress fabulously floated down the red carpet in the trending color of the night.

Taylor Zakhar Perez in Prada

The breakout star of Red, White, and Royal Blue rocked a light blue hue courtesy of his custom Prada suit. Perez proved that even leading men can look dashing in softer shades; other actors, take note!

Da'Vine Joy Randolph in Louis Vuitton

The winner for Best Supporting Actress looked joyful in a pale blue hand-embroidered tulle gown with oversized sleeves embellished with fringe with pale blue satin sandals, custom designed for her by the house of Louis Vuitton.

Florence Pugh in Del Core

Pugh chose a sci-fi-inspired look from the buzzy Italian brand Del Core. The look featured an alien-like sequined bodice with sharp straps and points at the peplum. The star's silk skirt continued the stream of the soft seafoam shade.

Ava DuVernay in Louis Vuitton

The director looked dramatic in a custom Louis Vuitton blue dress that featured long sleeves and a buckle strap detail at the shoulders.
