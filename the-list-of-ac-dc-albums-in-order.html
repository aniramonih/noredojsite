The List of AC/DC Albums in Order

Posted by Kelle Repass on Sunday, August 25, 2024

AC/DC is an iconic Australian rock band that has been active since 1973. Known for their high-energy performances and gritty sound, AC/DC has released numerous albums throughout their career. This article presents a comprehensive list of AC/DC albums in order, starting from their earliest releases to their latest.

1. High Voltage (1975)
Released in 1975 in Australia and 1976 internationally, “High Voltage” was AC/DC’s debut studio album. It featured tracks like “It’s a Long Way to the Top (If You Wanna Rock ‘n’ Roll)” and “T.N.T.”.

2. T.N.T. (1975)
Originally released in Australia as a live album, “T.N.T.” was later reissued as a studio album in 1976 for international markets. It featured famous tracks such as “Rock ‘n’ Roll Singer” and “The Jack”.

3. Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap (1976)
With hits like the title track “Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap” and “Problem Child,” this album showcased AC/DC’s driving sound and established them as international rock stars.

4. Let There Be Rock (1977)
Considered a cornerstone of AC/DC’s discography, “Let There Be Rock” truly solidified the band’s signature sound. The album features unforgettable tracks like “Whole Lotta Rosie” and “Dog Eat Dog.”

5. Powerage (1978)
“Powerage” introduced a slightly darker and more mature sound for AC/DC. Tracks such as “Sin City” and “Down Payment Blues” demonstrated the band’s ability to evolve while staying true to their roots.

6. Highway to Hell (1979)
Regarded as one of AC/DC’s greatest achievements, “Highway to Hell” propelled the band to international success. With anthems like the title track “Highway to Hell” and “Girls Got Rhythm,” the album reached No. 17 on the U.S. Billboard 200 chart.

7. Back in Black (1980)
Following the tragic death of lead singer Bon Scott, AC/DC released “Back in Black” with new vocalist Brian Johnson. The album became an instant classic, featuring iconic tracks such as “Hells Bells” and “You Shook Me All Night Long.” It went on to become one of the best-selling albums of all time, with over 50 million copies sold worldwide.

8. For Those About to Rock We Salute You (1981)
Continuing their momentum, AC/DC released this album which reached No. 1 on the U.S. Billboard 200 chart. The title track “For Those About to Rock (We Salute You)” became a fan favorite and a staple of their live shows.

9. Flick of the Switch (1983)
“Flick of the Switch” marked a return to a more stripped-down rock sound for AC/DC. The title track and “Guns for Hire” are just two of the standout songs from this album.

10. Fly on the Wall (1985)
“Fly on the Wall” received mixed reviews upon its release but still contained some solid rock tracks, including “Shake Your Foundations” and “Sink the Pink.”

11. Blow Up Your Video (1988)
With the intention of capturing their trademark sound, AC/DC delivered “Blow Up Your Video.” The album featured the hits “Heatseeker” and “That’s the Way I Wanna Rock ‘n’ Roll.”

12. The Razors Edge (1990)
“The Razors Edge” saw AC/DC return to the mainstream charts with hits like “Thunderstruck” and “Moneytalks.” It became one of their best-selling albums of the ’90s.

13. Ballbreaker (1995)
After a five-year break, AC/DC returned with “Ballbreaker.” The album received positive reviews and featured tracks like “Hard as a Rock” and “Hail Caesar.”

14. Stiff Upper Lip (2000)
Released in 2000, “Stiff Upper Lip” showcased AC/DC’s ability to maintain their classic sound and rock ‘n’ roll spirit. The title track and “Safe in New York City” proved to be successful singles.

15. Black Ice (2008)
“Black Ice” marked AC/DC’s first studio album in eight years. It debuted at No. 1 in 29 countries and featured tracks such as “Rock ‘n’ Roll Train” and “Big Jack.”

16. Rock or Bust (2014)
This album marked the departure of founding member and rhythm guitarist Malcolm Young due to illness. Nevertheless, AC/DC displayed their resilience by delivering hard-hitting tracks like “Play Ball” and the title track “Rock or Bust.”

These are the studio albums released by AC/DC in chronological order, showcasing their evolution and impact on the rock music scene throughout the decades.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How many albums has AC/DC released?
AC/DC has released a total of 16 studio albums.

2. Which is AC/DC’s most successful album?
AC/DC’s most successful album to date is “Back in Black,” which has sold over 50 million copies worldwide.

3. When was AC/DC’s debut album released?
AC/DC’s debut studio album, “High Voltage,” was released in 1975 in Australia and 1976 internationally.

4. Who is the lead guitarist of AC/DC?
Angus Young is the lead guitarist of AC/DC and is known for his energetic performances and iconic schoolboy uniform.

5. What is AC/DC’s best-selling single?
AC/DC’s best-selling single is “Highway to Hell” from their 1979 album of the same name. It remains a fan favorite and a staple in their live performances.

6. Has AC/DC ever won a Grammy Award?
Yes, AC/DC won their first and only Grammy Award in 2010 for “Best Hard Rock Performance” with the song “War Machine” from their album “Black Ice.”

7. How many records has AC/DC sold worldwide?
AC/DC has sold over 200 million records worldwide, making them one of the best-selling music artists of all time.

8. Who were the original members of AC/DC?
The original members of AC/DC were Malcolm Young, Angus Young, Bon Scott, Mark Evans, and Phil Rudd.

9. Who replaced Bon Scott as the lead vocalist of AC/DC?
After the death of Bon Scott in 1980, Brian Johnson joined AC/DC as the lead vocalist.

10. Which AC/DC album was the first to feature Brian Johnson as the lead vocalist?
“Back in Black,” released in 1980, was the first AC/DC album to feature Brian Johnson as the lead vocalist.

11. How many songs from AC/DC have reached the top of the charts?
AC/DC has had three songs reach the number one spot on the U.S. Mainstream Rock chart: “Moneytalks,” “Thunderstruck,” and “Rock ‘n’ Roll Train.”

12. What is AC/DC’s most recent studio album?
AC/DC’s most recent studio album is “Rock or Bust,” released in 2014.

These frequently asked questions should provide valuable information to fans and those wanting to learn more about AC/DC’s albums, success, and history.
