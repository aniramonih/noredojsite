FAQs about Esther Rantzen

Who is Esther Rantzen?

Esther Rantzen is a well-known British journalist and television presenter, famous for creating and hosting the BBC show That’s Life! which aired for 21 years. She has also made significant contributions to charity, founding Childline and The Silver Line.

What was That’s Life! about?

It was a consumer affairs television program that combined serious investigative journalism with light-hearted features and humorous segments. It was renowned for addressing various social issues and became one of the BBC’s most popular shows.

What is Childline?

Childline is a 24-hour helpline for children in distress or danger, which she founded in 1986. It offers confidential counseling and support to young people and has been instrumental in helping millions of children across the UK.

What is The Silver Line?

The Silver Line is a helpline established by Esther Rantzen in 2013 to combat loneliness among older people. It provides information, advice, and friendship through phone calls, helping to alleviate the isolation felt by many elderly individuals.

How has Esther Rantzen contributed to charity?

Beyond founding Childline and The Silver Line, she has been involved in numerous charitable activities. She has campaigned for various causes, including better hospice care and awareness of chronic fatigue syndrome.

What are some of Esther Rantzen’s personal achievements?

She has been awarded multiple honors, including the BAFTA Special Award for Journalism and several honorary doctorates. She was also made a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire (DBE) for her services to broadcasting and charity.

Where does she live now?

She moved to the New Forest during the COVID-19 lockdown and has expressed great joy in her new countryside life. She enjoys spending time with her grandchildren in her six-bedroom farmhouse.

What is Esther Rantzen’s connection to assisted dying?

She has been a vocal advocate for assisted dying, especially following her diagnosis of stage 4 lung cancer. She has publicly supported changes in legislation to allow assisted dying in the UK.

What books has Esther Rantzen written?
