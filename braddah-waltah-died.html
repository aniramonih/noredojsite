Braddah Waltah Died: Know Everything About Him

Posted by Kelle Repass on Friday, August 16, 2024

Bruddah Waltah Aipolani, a resident of Hilo, Hawaii, was a remarkable figure in the world of Hawaiian Music and a dedicated leader at Native Village Rescue. On an unexpected note, he passed away on Thursday, August 17, 2023. As we delve further into this article, we’ll uncover more about Braddah Waltah’s life, who he was, and the circumstances surrounding his passing.

Who Was Braddah Walter?

Braddah Walter Aipolani, a native of Hawaii, left a lasting legacy as a versatile artist and musician. Born in Keaukaha, a region near Hilo, he showcased his musical talents as a singer, guitarist, and songwriter. Braddah Waltah also demonstrated leadership skills, serving as a team leader at Native Village Rescue, a role in which he undoubtedly made a positive impact. His involvement in the arts extended beyond his musical endeavors; he once held the position of Observer at Rithem Entertainment Group and worked at Luvin Life. Notably, from June 2009 to 2014, he enthralled audiences as a Singer/Entertainer at KALEO’S BAR & GRILL-PAHOA.

His educational journey included studies in Life Sciences at the College of Life following his graduation from Waianae High School. Braddah Waltah was deeply rooted in his home state, residing in Hilo, Hawaii, and sharing his life with Thailiana Denise Leionaona Aipolani, whom he married on November 24, 1977.

Braddah Waltah Died: What Was His Cause of Death?

Braddah Waltah, fondly known as the “Godfather of Hawaiian Reggae,” peacefully passed away following a prolonged battle with declining health. On Thursday, he took his last breath, surrounded by his closest family, at his residence in Hilo. His renowned song, “Sweet Lady of Waiahole,” has graced the airwaves of 92.3 KSSK for many years, becoming a beloved favorite. Braddah Waltah found immense joy in being an entertainer, sharing his voice and music with the world. His affection and melodies have touched the lives of countless individuals. Although he is no longer with us, his musical legacy will endure, keeping his spirit alive.
