Brian Quinn Impractical Jokers

Impractical Jokers is a hidden camera reality show with operatic features which airs in the United States. Impractical Jokers, produced by NorthSouth Productions, premiered on truTV on December 15, 2011, starring Joe Gatto, James “Murr” Murray, Brian “Q” Quinn, and Sal Vulcano, four members of The Tenderloins. TBS also broadcasts it as well.

Joe Gatto, James “Murr” Murray, Brian “Q” Quinn, and Sal Vulcano, four friends from Staten Island’s Monsignor Farrell High School, formed the live improv and sketch comedy troupe known as ‘The Tenderloins’ in 1999.

On April 12, 2011, eight months before the show’s premiere, TruTV revealed Impractical Jokers, which was originally titled Mission: Uncomfortable. Murray demonstrated how the jokesters’ abilities made the hidden camera format make sense. “We wanted to find the right format… the thing is, we’ve been doing this for years, but the humiliation is exacerbated when it’s on tape.”

Brian Quinn Say Whaaat?

This sporadic podcast, hosted by Sal Vulcano and Quinn, was named Best New Show at the 2013 Stitcher Awards. Joe Gatto and James Murray decided to start their own Tenderloins podcast without the other two members due to the growing popularity of Say Whaaat?
