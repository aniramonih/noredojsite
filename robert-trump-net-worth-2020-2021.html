Early Life

Robert Trump is the youngest of all his brothers. His father, Fred Trump, who was born in New York, was one of the biggest real estate developers in New York City. Fred’s father and Robert’s grandfather, Frederick Trump, made a fortune during the Gold Rush.

With this huge inheritance, Fred Trump made a company and named it after his mother, Elizabeth Trump & Son. Years later, when Donald Trump inherited the family assets and became the Chairman and President, he renamed the company as The Trump Organization. He continued to occupy this position until he became the President of the US.

Robert Trump was born two years after Donald Trump, who was born on June 14, 1946, in 1948, in the Queens area in New York. Unlike Donald, who enrolled in the New York Military Academy and later studied at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, not much is known about the academic life of Robert Trump. However, one can be certain that his early education must have taken place in a prominent school.

Personal Life

Robert Trump married Blaine Trump, and the marriage lasted a quarter of a century, or, for 25 years. Robert met Ann while she worked in the Trump family real estate office in Brooklyn. Ann Marie belongs to the family who owns and operates Gurney’s, which is a well-known resort and spa in Montauk, at one end of Long Island.

Career

Robert Trump became a top executive of The Trump Organization, which is the family’s real estate business. He was responsible for the Trump Organization’s real estate holdings outside of Manhattan and New York. Formerly it was named Elizabeth Trump & Son and was later renamed Trump Organization when Donald Trump became the President and Chairman of the company after his father, Fred, died in 1999.

Robert Trump is 70 years old and has retired from an active role in the office of the Trump Organization. He lives in Millbrook, New York, with his partner Ann Marie Pallan in the $3.7 million houses which he gifted to Ann Marie in 2004, before his divorce.

After his retirement, Robert indulges in several philanthropic and community service activities. He also acts as an angel investor and mentors to entrepreneurs and not-for-profit organizations in the vicinity. He is also a trustee of the Angels of Light, a non-profit organization that supports and entertains people by providing holidays. He also donates to animals and especially horse rescue operations.

Awards & Achievement
