Ananta Ahuja and her dog killed in West Windsor car crash

Posted by Tandra Barner on Friday, August 30, 2024

West Windsor, NJ – A devastating car accident in West Windsor Township, NJ, resulted in the tragic loss of a young life and her faithful pet. 12-year-old Ananta Ahuja, a resident of West Windsor Township, and her dog lost their lives after being struck by a vehicle while walking near the intersection of Rabbit Hill Road and Abbington Lane.

The incident, occurring around 5:37 p.m. on Monday, involved a gray Jeep Grand Cherokee driven by a 65-year-old man. According to reports, Ananta Ahuja and her dog were hit while crossing the intersection, leading to fatal injuries for both. First responders rushed to the scene, offering immediate aid to Ananta, who was subsequently transported to Penn Medicine Princeton Medical Center. Unfortunately, she succumbed to her injuries, while her dog passed away at the accident site.

Authorities from the West Windsor Police Department and the Mercer County Prosecutor’s Office Serious Collision Response Team are conducting an investigation into the tragic incident. The driver of the Jeep remained at the scene following the collision, and their identity is currently being withheld as part of the ongoing investigation.

This heart-wrenching incident has left the community in mourning over the loss of young Ananta Ahuja and her cherished pet. The entire community extends its deepest sympathies to the Ahuja family and mourns the loss of a beloved child in this unfortunate and heartbreaking accident.
