Kathryn Kuhlman was an American evangelist and faith healer who gained international fame for her healing ministry and charismatic preaching. Kuhlman’s powerful sermons and miraculous healings attracted thousands of followers worldwide, and she became a leading figure in the Charismatic Movement of the 20th century. Her legacy continues to inspire millions of people today.

  • 1907: Kathryn Kuhlman was born in Concordia, Missouri, United States.
  • 1928: Kuhlman graduated from Bethany-Peniel College (now Southern Nazarene University) in Bethany, Oklahoma.
  • 1933: Kuhlman began working as an evangelist for the Evangelical Revival Church in Idaho.
  • 1946: Kuhlman began hosting her radio program, “Smiling Through,” which aired on over 500 stations across the United States.
  • 1947: Kuhlman began hosting her television program, “I Believe in Miracles,” aired on CBS and other networks.
  • 1950: Kuhlman held her first significant healing service at Carnegie Hall in New York City, drawing over 6,000 attendees.
  • 1954: Kuhlman began hosting annual healing crusades at the Shrine Auditorium in Los Angeles, which attracted tens of thousands of attendees.
  • 1975: Kuhlman suffered a heart attack and underwent open-heart surgery.
  • 1976: Kuhlman held her last public healing service at the Shrine Auditorium in Los Angeles before retiring from public ministry due to ill health.
  • 1977: Kuhlman died of heart failure in Tulsa, Oklahoma, at 69.
