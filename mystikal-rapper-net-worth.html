Early Life

Michael Lawrence Tyler was born on September 22, 1970, in New Orleans, Louisiana to Marie Tyler. His father died when he was 7 years old. His mother alone raised him and his two siblings, Michelle and Maurice.

Mystikal went to Cohen High School before joining the US Army as a combat engineer. From a very young age, he wanted to become a rapper so he performed for Run-DMC and Doug E. Fresh.

It was an outdoor event at the Treme Center, which drew the attention of Big Boy Record’s house producer, Leroy “Precise” Edwards, who offered Mystikal a music contract.

Music Career

Mystikal released his self-titled first album in 1994 through the indie label Big Boy Records. Following the release, he found himself clashing with fellow New Orleans rappers including U.N.L.V., B.G., and Lil Wayne.

He released “Mind of Mystikal” in 1995 after signing with Jive Records mostly consisting of tracks from his debut. Mystikal left Big Boy in 1996 while still under contract with Jive to join No Limit Records.

Then he appeared as a guest artist in numerous No Limit albums from 1997 to 1999. He left No Limit but remained with Jive after the label’s two albums were released. He released his album, Let’s Get Ready, which debuted at #1 in 2000.

Mystikal was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Rap Album in 2003 for his solo album Tarantula, which was released in 2001. In addition, Mystikal was nominated for Best Male Rap Performance in the same year.

He was jailed for sexual battery from 2004 until 2010. Soon after gaining back his freedom, he worked with Fiend, a former labelmate from No Limit, and R&B singer Lloyd. He also gave a performance at the 2011 Gathering of the Juggalos festival.
