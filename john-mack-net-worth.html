Career

John Mack started his career with Morgan Stanley as a salesman in 1972. He quickly rose through the ranks and became the head of Stanley’s Worldwide Taxable Fixed Income Division from 1985 to 1992. In 1993, Mack became the President of Morgan Stanley. However, in 2001, he left the company due to a power struggle with Phil Purcell. He then became the CEO of Credit Suisse First Boston. In 2005, Mack returned to Morgan Stanley as CEO and Chairman of the Board. During his tenure, he earned over $41 million in 2006.
