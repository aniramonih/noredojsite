Rose Byrne: Radiant beauty and effortless grace.

Rose Byrne was born on July 24, 1979 in Balmain, Sydney, Australia.

She is the daughter of Jane, a primary school administrator, and Robin Byrne, a semi-retired statistician and market researcher.

Byrne has Irish and Scottish ancestry.

She is the youngest of four children; she has an older brother, George, and two older sisters, Alice and Lucy.

Rose Byrne is 44 Years Old.

Notably, she stands at 5 feet 6 inches (168 cm) tall and weighs around 115 lbs (52 kg).

She has dark brown hair and light brown eyes. She is known for her natural beauty and elegant style.

Her Early Life, Education And Ethnicity

Rose Byrne grew up in a close-knit family in Balmain, a suburb of Sydney.

She was encouraged by one of her sisters to join the Australian Theatre for Young People at age eight, where she discovered her passion for acting.

She attended Balmain Public School, Hunters Hill High School, and Bradfield Senior College.

Notably, she also studied at The University of Sydney, but dropped out to pursue her acting career.

Rose Byrne has described her family as “agnostic”. Her mother was an atheist, while her father was more open-minded.

She said in a 2009 interview, “I’m not religious, I’m not romantic and I live purely by logic.

I make every decision by logic and sometimes that leads me to the right and sometimes to the wrong decision.”

Her Personal Life, Husband And Children

Rose Byrne has been in a long-term relationship with American actor Bobby Cannavale since 2012.

They met on the set of the movie Annie, where they played love interests.

Notably, they have two sons together: Rocco, born in 2016, and Rafa, born in 2017.

They live in Brooklyn, New York, with their children and Cannavale’s son from a previous marriage, Jake.

Rose Byrne has never been married, but she has been engaged twice.

She was engaged to Australian writer, actor, and director Brendan Cowell from 2003 to 2010.

They broke up due to the long distance between them, as Byrne was working in the US and Cowell was in Australia.

She was also engaged to Australian director Gregor Jordan from 1999 to 2002.

They met on the set of the movie Two Hands, where they co-starred with Heath Ledger.

Rose’s Professional Career

Rose Byrne made her screen debut in the movie Dallas Doll in 1994, when she was 15 years old.

She continued to act in Australian film and television throughout the 1990s, appearing in shows like Heartbreak High, Echo Point, and Wildside.

And movies like The Date, My Mother Frank, and The Goddess of 1967.

Additionally, she won the Volpi Cup for Best Actress at the Venice Film Festival in 2000 for her role in The Goddess of 1967.

Here she played a blind girl who travels across Australia with a Japanese man.

She made the transition to American cinema in 2002, when she played Dormé, a handmaiden to Padmé Amidala, in Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones.

She followed this with roles in Hollywood productions like Troy, where she played Briseis, a Trojan priestess who falls in love with Achilles.

28 Weeks Later, where she played Scarlet, a doctor who tries to contain a zombie outbreak; and Knowing, where she played Diana, a woman who helps a professor unravel a mysterious code.

She gained critical acclaim and recognition for her role as Ellen Parsons, a young lawyer who works for a ruthless mentor, in the legal thriller series Damages, which ran from 2007 to 2012.

She was nominated for two Emmy Awards and two Golden Globe Awards for her performance.

Notably, she also starred in the comedy series Get Him to the Greek, where she played Jackie Q, a pop star and ex-girlfriend of a rock star.

Bridesmaids, where she played Helen, a wealthy and snobbish rival of the maid of honor; and Spy, where she played Rayna Boyanov, a villainous arms dealer.

She also ventured into the genres of horror and superhero, starring in the Insidious franchise.

Here she played Renai Lambert, a mother who deals with supernatural threats; and the X-Men franchise.

Here she played Moira MacTaggert, a CIA agent and love interest of Professor X.

Remarkably:

She also voiced Leatherhead, a mutant alligator, in the animated series Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem.

She has also been involved in producing and writing, co-founding The Dollhouse Collective in 2015 with four other Australian women in the film industry.

The production company aims to create stories with strong female characters and perspectives.

She has also co-written and co-produced the comedy movie Seriously Red, where she plays a woman who dreams of becoming a Dolly Parton impersonator.

Net Worth and Sources of Income

Rose Byrne has an estimated net worth of $25 million.

She has earned her wealth from her successful acting and producing career, as well as from endorsements and sponsorships.

She has appeared in several commercials and campaigns for brands like Oroton, Max Factor, Calvin Klein, and David Jones.

Notably, she has also invested in real estate, owning properties in Sydney, London, and New York.

Philanthropic Ventures

Rose Byrne is also a philanthropist who supports various causes and charities.

She is an ambassador for UNICEF Australia, a global organization that works to protect and improve the lives of children and families.

She has visited UNICEF projects in Vietnam, Laos, and the Philippines, and has participated in fundraising and awareness campaigns.

Furthermore, she is also a patron of the Sydney Theatre Company, a leading Australian theatre company that produces and presents contemporary and classic works.

She has performed in several plays for the company, such as Speed-the-Plow, Three Sisters, and You Can’t Take It with You.

Notably, she is also a supporter of the Breast Cancer Research Foundation, a nonprofit organization that funds research and awareness for breast cancer.

She has donated and auctioned items for the cause, and has attended events and galas.
