Von Miller Children: Meet Valor, The Son Of Von Miller

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, August 24, 2024

American football outside linebacker, Von Miller was born on March 26th, 1989 in Dallas, Texas in the United States of America.

He was born to Gloria Miller and Von Miller. He shares the same parents with his brother, Vince Miller. He also has a son named Valor Miller.

He ran track and played high school football for the DeSoto Eagles while attending DeSoto High School in DeSoto, Texas. He made 37 tackles, 14 tackles for loss, 7 sacks, and 12 quarterback hurries in his junior season.

In his senior year, he recorded 76 tackles, 14 tackles for loss, and 6 sacks, earning him the title of District 8-5A Defensive MVP. He played football alongside Cyrus Gray, an upcoming Aggie.

Miller participated in track and field events like the 110-meter hurdles (personal record of 14.38 seconds), triple leap (12.65 meters), and javelin throw (PR of 37.24 m) Miller

, rated as a four-star prospect by Rivals.com, was ranked as the nation’s No. 15 weakside defensive end in 2007.

Miller played football for the Texas A&M Aggies from 2007 to 2010 while he was a student at Texas A&M University. He recorded 22 tackles as a defensive end rookie in 2007, including 10 solo stops, and was awarded Freshman All-Big 12 by The Sporting News.

A second-round pick in the 2010 NFL Draft was predicted for Miller if he had forgone his senior year. He went into the 2011 NFL Draft as one of the top 3-4 outside linebackers available, and top-five draft pick projections were made for him.

Miller won the award for Defensive MVP in the 2011 Senior Bowl. At the 2011 NFL Combine, Miller finished second among linebackers in the 40-yard dash, third in the vertical jump, first in the broad jump, third in the 20-yard shuttle, first in the 3-cone drill, and first in the 60-yard shuttle.

Miller was chosen to represent elite rookies in the NFL Labor Union negotiations in March 2011. LaDainian Tomlinson, a former NFL running back, convinced him to sign on to the lawsuit.

The Denver Broncos selected Miller with the second overall pick in the 2011 NFL Draft. Since LaVar Arrington was taken second overall by the Washington Redskins in 2000, he was the highest-drafted linebacker. In addition, he became the Aggies’ top draft pick since 1992, when Quentin Coryatt was chosen second overall.

Miller forced a fumble in his debut play from scrimmage against the Oakland Raiders on September 12, 2011. When the Denver Broncos played the Cincinnati Bengals on September 18, 2011, he recorded his first sack of Andy Dalton.

Miller had 10 sacks in nine games in his second season, which was a strong start. He hurried the quarterback 30 times during this time frame, which was more than he did the whole 2011 season. Because of this outstanding start, he was an MVP contender.

Miller has been selected for eight Pro Bowls and has been named to the first team of the All-Pro team three times and the second team four times. He had 110.5 career sacks as of 2022, the most in Denver Broncos history and the most of any active player.

Miller received the 2015 Super Bowl MVP award in Super Bowl 50, which marked the end of the NFL season. Miller won the Super Bowl LVI with the Los Angeles Rams after being traded to them in 2021.

Von Miller: Meet Valor. the son of Von Miller

Valor Miller is the son of Von Miller. His mother is Megan Denise. He was born in 2021. His parents are no longer a couple.
