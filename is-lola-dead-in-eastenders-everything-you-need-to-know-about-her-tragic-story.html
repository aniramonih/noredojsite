Is Lola Dead in EastEnders? Everything You Need to Know About Her Tragic Story

Posted by Billy Koelling on Saturday, August 10, 2024

If you are a fan of EastEnders, you might be wondering what happened to Lola Pearce, the young hairdresser who was diagnosed with a terminal brain tumour.

Sadly, Lola passed away on Wednesday 31st May 2023, leaving behind her daughter Lexi and her husband Jay.

In this article, I will tell you everything you need to know about Lola’s tragic story and how it affected her loved ones.

Lola’s Diagnosis

Lola was rushed to hospital in October of 2022, after a series of dizzy spells and a serious seizure left her loved ones concerned.

She was diagnosed with a brain tumour and was told by doctors that her condition was incurable.

According to The Sun, actress Danielle Harold said that she was “honoured” to be trusted with such a “heartbreaking” storyline that “many people can relate to”.

Lola faced her diagnosis with bravery and optimism, and tried to make the most of her remaining time with her family and friends.

She also decided to reconnect with her biological mother Emma Harding (Patsy Kensit), who had abandoned her when she was three years old.

Lola hoped to find some closure and forgiveness from her mother, but Emma turned out to be selfish and manipulative, and tried to take advantage of Lola’s situation.

Lola’s Wedding

One of Lola’s biggest wishes was to marry Jay Brown (Jamie Borthwick), the love of her life and the father of her daughter Lexi (Isabella Brown).

Jay had been by Lola’s side every step of the way, and proposed to her on Christmas Day 2022.

Lola accepted his proposal and they planned a simple but beautiful wedding for January 2023.

The wedding day was full of joy and tears, as Lola and Jay exchanged their vows in front of their closest friends and family.

Lola looked stunning in a white dress and a flower crown, while Jay wore a blue suit and a matching tie.

They shared their first dance to Ed Sheeran’s “Perfect”, and thanked everyone for their support and love.

Lola also gave a touching speech, where she said that she was “the luckiest girl in the world” to have Jay and Lexi in her life.

Is Lola Dead in EastEnders?

Lola’s health deteriorated rapidly after her wedding, as she suffered from more seizures, headaches, and memory loss.

She was admitted to hospice care in May 2023, where she received palliative treatment to ease her pain and discomfort.

She knew that she didn’t have long left, and tried to prepare herself and her loved ones for the inevitable.

On Wednesday 31st May 2023, Lola passed away peacefully in her sleep, with Jay holding her hand.

She was 28 years old.

Her death left everyone heartbroken and devastated, especially Jay and Lexi, who struggled to cope with their loss.

Jay blamed himself for not being able to save Lola, while Lexi felt angry and confused about why her mummy had to die.

Lola’s Funeral

Lola’s funeral took place on Friday 9th June 2023, at Walford Crematorium.

It was attended by many of her friends and family from Albert Square, as well as some surprise guests from her past, such as Billy Mitchell (Perry Fenwick), Ben Mitchell (Max Bowden), Peter Beale (Dayle Hudson), and Abi Branning (Lorna Fitzgerald).

Everyone wore something pink or purple, which were Lola’s favourite colours.

The funeral service was led by Reverend Stevens (Michael Keating), who praised Lola for her kindness, courage, and humour.

He also read out some messages from people who couldn’t attend the funeral, such as Phil Mitchell (Steve McFadden), Honey Mitchell (Emma Barton), Denise Fox (Diane Parish), and Kim Fox (Tameka Empson). Jay delivered a moving eulogy, where he said that Lola was his “soulmate”, his “best friend”, and his “hero”.

He also promised to look after Lexi and make Lola proud.

After the service, everyone gathered at The Queen Vic pub for a wake, where they shared their memories and stories of Lola.

They also watched a video montage of Lola’s life, which featured clips from her childhood, her time in EastEnders, and her wedding day.

The video ended with a message from Lola herself, where she thanked everyone for their love and said that she would always be with them in spirit.
