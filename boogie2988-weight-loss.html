How Much Weight Did He Lose After Surgery?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Tuesday, August 13, 2024

Boogie2988 underwent a significant weight loss of 100 pounds in 2017 after having gastric bypass surgery. The YouTuber was 500 pounds before and his goal was to reach 300. However, unfortunately for him, Boogie2988 never reached his goal weight and has been unable to maintain his weight loss, he relapsed and gave in to the eating disorder he said he had, and now, he weighs 391.6 pounds.

Steven Williams, who is better known by his online alias Boogie2988 or simply Boogie, came into prominence with his video rants on Youtube about video games and nerd culture as a character named Francis. As the Youtuber got more popular in the online community, he started getting personal with his followers and began sharing about his weight loss journey except he never made any effort to lose weight.

He had gastric bypass surgery in 2017 which made it seem like he was very sincere about getting in shape and being healthy but that was all he did. He did not even change his lifestyle to maintain his body afterward and ended up gaining weight again. Let's discuss Boogie2988's weight loss journey!

Boogie2988's Weight Loss Surgery: He Lost 100 Pounds After Undergoing Gastric Bypass Surgery!

Boogie2988 (@realboogie2988), the Youtuber whose real name is Steven Williams, underwent a significant weight loss of 100 pounds in 2017 after having gastric bypass surgery. He was 500 pounds before he had the surgery and he lost about 50 pounds after starting a diet. In the boxing match he had recently, he seems to have put on weight again.

Boogie2988 has always struggled with his weight. He was just another fat kid when he was a child but as he grew up, his weight issues got worse and by the time he was an adult, he was morbidly obese. He weighed 500 pounds. He looked massive and he was aware that he was massive every instant because 500 pounds is a huge burden to carry and it became an inconvenience even while doing the simplest of tasks like walking. He seriously needed to embark on a weight loss journey.

And Boogie2988 said that he was on a weight loss journey. But because he weighed the same for the longest of time and never lost weight, it was hard to believe he was doing something about his morbid obesity. In 2012, the status quo changed but he did not lose weight, instead, he gained. When fans got concerned, he said that the weight gain was minimal and it just paused the 'weight loss.' It did not look like he was ever serious about losing weight.

But 2017 came and Boogie2988 underwent gastric bypass surgery. He had already been on a diet and lost 50 pounds before he had the operation. After the operation, he saw a 100 pounds drop and that was the most significant weight loss he had ever had. To reach his goal weight of 300 pounds, he had 50 more pounds to lose, and his fans thought, 'what's 50 more?' because he finally looked determined to keep his weight in check. He did say he was worried about his life cutting short because of morbid obesity and that he was stupidly motivated to not have that happen.

Boogie2988 could not maintain his weight loss after having the surgery.

But Boogie2988 never reached his goal weight of 300 pounds and in fact, he could not even retain his weight loss. That's right, he undid everything that the gastric bypass surgery did. He gained again. And he was criticized by his followers for that. Right now, he weighs 391.6 pounds and he is about 100 pounds from his goal weight but it doesn't look like he will ever bridge the gap because every time his weight comes up, he starts making excuses for it.

Not that anybody's weight should be our concern and we should be criticizing others for gaining weight, but because Boogie2988 has constantly tried to position himself as a weight loss influencer, ever since he had the operation, fans have become too invested in it and now, they are disappointed in him. They didn't like how he didn't change his lifestyle after having the surgery because how could surgery help if he didn't actually make a change himself? It only gives people a headstart and there is the rest of the journey to go on.
