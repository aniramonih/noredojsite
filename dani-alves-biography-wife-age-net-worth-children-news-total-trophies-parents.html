Biography

Daniel Alves (born 6 May 1983), also known as Dani Alves, was a renowned professional footballer celebrated for his role as a right-back. 

His remarkable career spanned prestigious clubs, including Barcelona, Juventus, and Paris Saint-Germain. Although he bid farewell to club football in February 2024, his dedication to representing Brazil persisted until March 2023. 

Dani Alves was revered for his adept attacking prowess and exemplary leadership qualities, evident in his captaincy on the field. His retirement garnered widespread admiration and respect from fans and peers alike.

Early Life & Education

Dani Alves, a professional footballer, was born on 6 May 1983 in Juazeiro, State of Bahia, Brazil. He grew up with his parents, Domingos Alves Da Silva and Dona Lucia, alongside his brother Ney Alves. 

Dani Alves completed his secondary education at Lycée Albert 1er in Monaco. His early life and education have shaped him into the successful athlete and person he is today.

Career

Dani Alves kicked off his incredible football journey in 2001 with Bahia in Brazil, laying the groundwork for an extraordinary career. His leap to Sevilla in Spain a year later marked a turning point, where he tasted success with two UEFA Cups and the Copa del Rey across six memorable seasons. 

But his switch to Barcelona in 2008 truly defined his legacy. Over eight unforgettable years, Dani racked up an impressive collection of trophies, including multiple La Liga titles, Champions League glories, and Copa del Rey triumphs. Despite brief stints at Juventus and Paris Saint-Germain, he returned to his roots in 2019, donning the São Paulo jersey until his retirement in 2021.

Dani Alves‘ legend endures despite a few bumps along the road, like his brief stint with Pumas UNAM in Mexico in 2022. He proudly holds the title of Brazil’s third most-capped player, representing his country from 2006 to 2022.

 His international resume boasts three World Cup appearances and five Copa América tournaments, with wins in 2007 and 2019. Dani Alves also clinched victories in the Confederations Cups of 2009 and 2013 and a cherished gold medal at the 2020 Olympics. Dani Alves has left an indelible mark as one of the game’s greatest icons through these triumphs.

Personal Life

Dani Alves, aged 41, has had some challenges in his personal life. He has been married twice, with his first marriage to Dinora Santana in 2008. The couple had two children together, a daughter, Victoria Alves, and a son, Daniel Alves, but unfortunately, the marriage ended in 2011. 

In 2017, Dani Alves married Spanish model Joana Sanz. However, their relationship ended in 2023 due to Alves‘ legal troubles.

Dani Alves was romantically linked with Thaíssa Carvalho from 2012 to 2014. Rumors suggest connections with Fernanda Penido in 2014 and Carol Macedo in 2011.

Rape Case

Dani Alves, a professional soccer player, was recently convicted of rape and sentenced to prison in Spain. Despite the serious allegations against him, his first wife, Dinora Santana, has publicly supported him. 

However, it has been reported that his second wife, Joana Sanz, has ended their marriage due to the rape allegations. This situation highlights the complex and controversial nature of supporting someone accused of a serious crime.
