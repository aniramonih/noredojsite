Leo Anthony Gallagher Jr., born July 24, 1946, and died November 11, 2022, was an American comedian who became one of the most recognizable comedic performers of the 1980s for his prop and observational routine that included the signature act of smashing a watermelon on stage with a sledgehammer. For more than 30 years, he performed between 100 and 200 shows per year, destroying tens of thousands of melons with the sledgehammer he dubbed the “Sledge-O-Matic.”

Gallagher began working as the road manager for comic/musician Jim Stafford after graduating from college. Stafford and Gallagher went to California in 1969, and Gallagher decided to perform himself. While frequenting The Comedy Store and The Ice House, he began honing his own comedy act. He appeared twice on The Tonight Show with Johnny Carson, despite Carson’s dislike of prop comedy. Gallagher appeared on the show for the first time on December 5, 1975, when he demonstrated his prop, “The Tonight Show Home Game,” and then again on May 9, 1979. Gallagher also appeared on the show several times when guest hosts were filling in for Carson. Gallagher also appeared on The Merv Griffin Show and The Mike Douglas Show. He appeared at several Playboy Clubs.
