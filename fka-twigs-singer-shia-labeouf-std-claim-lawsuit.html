Did Shia LaBeouf give FKA Twigs an STD?

Posted by Billy Koelling on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

BRITISH songstress FKA Twigs claimed in a lawsuit that former boyfriend Shia LaBeouf, gave her an STD during their relationship.

She also went on to further claim that he was abusive in several ways towards her.

Did FKA Twigs accuse Shia LaBeouf of giving her an STD?

According to the lawsuit obtained by the New York Times, FKA Twigs - whose real name is Tahliah Debrett Barnett - claimed the Transformers star gave her an STD.

The singer, 32, and the troubled actor, 34, were together for a year until they split in 2019.

She claimed that he "knowingly" transmitted the infection to her.

The actor has denied all allegations made by Twigs through his reps.

What are sexually transmitted diseases?

Sexually transmitted diseases (STD), or sexually transmitted infections (STI), are "infections that are passed from one person to another through sexual contact," according to Medline Plus.

The contact can be spread through vaginal, oral or anal sex.

Some diseases, like herpes or HPV, can be spread through skin-to-skin contact.

Different types of STD include chlamydia, genital herpes, gonorrhea, HIV/AIDS, pubic lice, and syphilis.

STDs can be caused by bacteria, viruses, and parasites. Some STD's do not display symptoms, while most do.

What other accusations did FKA Twigs make against Shia LaBeouf?

Twigs also claimed in the lawsuit that LaBeouf abused her "physically, emotionally, and mentally many times in a relationship that lasted just short of a year."

She accused him of "relentless abuse," including sexual battery, assault, and infliction of emotional distress.

FKA Twigs - who met the actor on the set of his autobiographical movie Honey Boy in 2018 - also alleged in the lawsuit that he would squeeze or grab her to the point of bruising, but that she didn't go to the police out of concern for his career.

In the lawsuit, FKA Twigs detailed a terrifying incident on February 14, 2019, when he allegedly assaulted her.

She claimed Shia was "driving recklessly" with her in the car.

Shia told The New York Times in an email: "I’m not in any position to tell anyone how my behavior made them feel.

"I have no excuses for my alcoholism or aggression, only rationalizations. I have been abusive to myself and everyone around me for years.

"I have a history of hurting the people closest to me. I’m ashamed of that history and am sorry to those I hurt. There is nothing else I can really say."

The singer is also speaking out about Shia in her first-ever television interview.

"He would often just start having an argument with me in the middle of the night, start accusing me of doing all sorts of things, planning to leave him in my head," she told Gayle King in an interview that will air on CBS on February 18, 2021.
