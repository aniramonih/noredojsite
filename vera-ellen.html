In 1977 a gossip columnist reported that the baby Vera-Ellen lost in the 60s had died from SIDS.

Married to millionaire oilman Andrew Rothschild he started a divorce suite against her in August 1966.

On March 3, 1963, she gave birth to a daughter named Victoria Ellen Rothschild, who died of Sudden Infant Death Syndrome on June 20th of that same year. All of this took place in Los Angeles County.

She was originally slated to star in Excuse My Dust (1951) with Red Skelton, however she had to be replaced as she was in London filming Happy Go Lovely - Color - 1951 (1951) and would not finish that film in time.

She danced with Fred Astaire in Three Little Words (1950) and with Gene Kelly in On the Town (1949). Blonde, slim of build, and a dancing sensation, she appeared in a string of light-hearted but successful films.

Vera-Ellen began dancing at the age of 10, and a few years later became one of the youngest Rockettes. She appeared in several Broadway musicals until she was spotted by film producer Samuel Goldwyn in 1945.

She was only 24 years old when Goldwyn cast her opposite Danny Kaye in Wonder Man (1945).

She was known to have the smallest waist in Hollywood from the mid 1940s through the 1960s.

It has been reported that she appeared in "Major Bowes' Amateur Theatre of the Air" in 1935, but that remains unconfirmed.
